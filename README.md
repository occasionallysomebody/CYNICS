## Why "Messy" Notes Are Good For Research
A good friend and fellow researcher taught me:

"Research is a non-linear. You're actually encouraged to go down rabbit holes! Research is different from school because there are no pre-defined answers. Often, you don't know if the problem you're approaching is even solvable in the first place.

The more time you spend on constructing or working in parallel on a final product, the slower and bogged down you become.

You want to be able to explore and learn in all directions including tangents."

## How To Read A Paper
##### Order of Reading:
1. Abstract
2. Conclusion
3. Intro

Research papers are not novels. They're not designed to be read front-to-back.

Whenever you come across a word you don't understand, stop everything and look into that topic.

Taking down questions you have which may not be relevant to the paper completely should be looked at when you finish that paragraph.
